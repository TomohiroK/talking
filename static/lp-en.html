Guide

How to use EchoTalk

Everything you need to know, all in one place.

When you open the app, you'll see the character selection screen.

1. Choose the language you want to practice (English, Indonesian, or Japanese).

2. Select an age range. The AI adapts its vocabulary and tone to match.

3. Tap on Angie or Andy. The countdown begins, and you're off!

When prompted, allow microphone access in your browser.

Short sentences are fine. You don't need perfect grammar. The AI understands you. Just start talking.

Ask questions. Try "What do you think about...?" or "Tell me about..." to get the AI talking.

Don't fear mistakes. The AI is a friend, not a teacher. It'll naturally correct you in conversation.

Mix languages. The AI understands Japanese input. Say something in Japanese if you can't find the English words, and learn from the AI's response.

In English mode, Japanese translations appear in small gray text below each AI response.

Use translations as a safety net — try to understand the English first, then check if you got it right.

Translations don't appear in Japanese mode since the AI already responds in Japanese.

The word panel appears on the right side of the screen (on mobile, tap the button in the bottom-right corner).

Words from AI responses are automatically listed. Tap any word to bookmark it — it'll be highlighted in yellow.

Bookmarked words stay saved throughout the conversation. Mark any new words you encounter.

You can't change language or age during a conversation. Tap the "← Back" button in the top-left to return to the selection screen.

Age levels explained:

👶 ~5 years: Very simple words. Animals, colors, food topics.

🍦 7-15 years: Easy vocabulary. Games, school, sports topics.

🚀 16-25 years: Natural conversation. Trends, social media, career topics.

💬 25+ years: No restrictions. Free-flowing conversation.

Mic not working: Check your browser's microphone permission. Click the mic icon in the address bar and select "Allow".

Can't hear the AI: Check your device volume. Make sure headphones or speakers are connected properly.

Connection dropped: Reload the page and select your character again.

Audio cutting out: Wi-Fi is recommended. Mobile data may cause instability.
